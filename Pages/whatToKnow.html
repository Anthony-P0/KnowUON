What to Know by Week 1?

By week 1 at the University of Newcastle new students will need to know important information. This information will be vital to starting and completing your first semester, as well as helping avoid unnecessary stress.

Here is What You Need to Know by Week 1:

Understand what you will be learning in your enrolled courses, what the course will involve (assessments, assignments, etc.) and, what will be expected from you. All can be found in your Course Outline on Blackboard.



Make sure you know where your classes (lectures, tutorials, etc.) will be held, and make sure you know when your classes will run. Your timetable on myTimetable will provide all this information.



Make sure you know who the important people are during your time at university. These people will include Program Convenors, Course Coordinators, and anyone involved in your program and courses. Please use our Directory.



Blackboard (UONline) is an online system/tool used for learning. It provides the learning resources and technologies needed for you to learn the skills and knowledge of your courses.



Make sure you know your way around and important locations on your campus. These locations will help you learn, stay healthy and stress free, and allow you to take time out from study. These locations can be found on our Interactive Map and our Support Services page.



Make sure you know the supports and services available to you. These supports and services can be found online and on-campus. They will help you get through your time at UON and make sure UON can suit your needs. These supports and services can be found on our Support and Services page.

What a Typical Semester Feels and Looks Like?

The first semester as a new student can cause you to feel many different emotions. You might feel excited about starting something new and at the same time could feel overwhelmed towards the same prospect. As a new student the semester can feel daunting as it can look like there will be a lot of work involved, but it can also be refreshing as the semester looks like a new challenge and change.

Starting a new semester can feel like it moves too fast or even slow which can cause frustration and anxiety as you think university is boring or too much to handle, leading to stress. But if you manage to get through the first few weeks of the semester, you will find yourself in a routine without all that stress. The start of a new semester can bring positive thoughts and emotions too. For example, learning new information about your area of study can make you feel a keen desire to learn and strive to succeed. And with this desire to learn you can feel like university is where you want to be.
