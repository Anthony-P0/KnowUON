Frequently Asked Questions

How should I start my studies?

First of all, freshmen should enroll your chosen course, and then plan your timetable according to your preferences. After the freshmen complete the registration, everyone can start formal learning. I suggest that every freshman starts by understanding the course settings of each course, and find the course you registered in Blackboard. Usually you can browse the outline of the course in the course catalog. Freshmen can quickly grasp the most important information of the course by reading the outline.


Is the University of Newcastle's website mobile compatible

Yes, just enter the university’s URLin your phone and the website will automatically adjust the format to fit your screen.

For information on available mobile apps from the University please see myUON Student Portal - information for students.

How to ask my course tutor for help?

Freshmen can seek help from the course coordinator or lecturer of the course when they encounter difficulties in the course. In class, tutorial or pass courses, it is best for students to ask questions and communicate with the teacher in real time. Or students can contact the tutor for help after class. Freshmen can usually find the tutor’s office address or phone or email contact information in Blackboard’s contact, or search for the tutor directly on the school website.

What is the assessment process of the course?

The assessment process of each course is different. Freshmen should browse the assessment method and time period of each course in time after registration. Freshmen can find the course setting information of the course in the Blackboard after enrolling, pay attention to the assessment method and time, especially the compulsory requirement of each course.

How can I seek further help?

Freshmen should contact the school for help in time if they encounter any problems. If students want to discuss course selection or course payment with the school, ask the school for specific arrangements. Students can contact the school through the contact information provided by the school's AskUonwebpage, including media software, telephone, live chat , or direct mail to contact the school.
