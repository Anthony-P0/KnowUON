Frequently Asked Questions

Students should follow their course handbook for their degree, course information can be found here by searching our degrees.
Plan your semester ahead of enrolling by searching the course you are interested in and writing your preferred times down. Afterwards when enrolment opens you will be prepared and ready for your upcoming semester!

A short time before your semester begins your course will appear on Blackboard , and you can prepare further for your course. It is recommended to read the Course Outline provided on each course and be aware of assignment deadlines and any further requisites stated in the outline.

Students can seek help from the course coordinator, lecturer and or the class tutor when they encounter difficulties in the course. Students can often ask questions on Blackboard using the Discussion Forums of the specified course. If the student does not wish to ask the question publicly they can contact their tutor or course coordinator using their email, often found on the Contacts page of Blackboard.

Students can find further help services by exploring the university's AskUon webpage, or contact via telephone, live chat , or they can send an email to contact the university.

The university offers a range of support services for new students. One that is strongly recommended is PASS (Peer Assisted Study Session). PASS is intended as an informal study session designed for specific 1000 and 2000 level courses where students can study in a relaxed environment led by previous students who can provide help with questions relating courseworks, provide tips for assignments etc. More information about PASS can be found at PASS.

The university has more than 100 registered clubs and societies, including sporting, cultural, social, philosophical, and academic fields. Freshmen can choose to join the club they are interested in, or create a club themselves Click here for more information.

Depending on the subject you are studying a laptop can come in handy. However given that not all financial situations are the same the university provides a range of computers throughout classrooms and the libraries. Students can freely use computers and equipment, as well as wireless and wired networks on campus.

In order to better integrate into school life, the school provides free Academic Learning Support courses for international students to improve their Academic Skills abilities, including researching, independent study skills, writing, critical reading, problem solving and logical thinking are essential for university, which can be a new and challenging experience. In addition, the school has organized an English corner to help international students improve their English skills. International freshmen can ask International Enquiries for relevant information after enrolling.

Changing majors can be completed by logging into myUON, going to Academic Records, clicking on Major and Study Choices and selecting a new major from selection list. Click submit and you have changed majors. If you have undertaken study in your program, please check your program plan and program handbook to ensure you are able to complete a new major. If you are unsure, please consult your Student Progress Advisor before enrolling in a new major.

For internal transfers of programs, students must complete an Internal Transfer application. Applications will usually open toward the end of the semester. Please look here for further information.

Students can find ticketed parking throughout the campus, tickets must be purchased at a ticketing machine and displayed behind the windshield of your car. Parking permits may also be purchased if you do not want to use the tickets, click here to view further information on purchasing parking permits. Car Park 14 and 16 are free parking zones on campus, students may choose to park there instead. For more detailed information on university parking, click this link.

The campus has several bus stops that public buses stop at. The campus also provides a NUspace shuttle service for travel between the Callaghan and Newcastle City campuses. For further information on the shuttle service please click here .
